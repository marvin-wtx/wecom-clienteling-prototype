# Prototype Quality Evaluation

Evaluate the delivered product first, then use the documents to locate evidence.

## Three practical checks

### 10-second first impression

Open the product in the review viewport. It must show a complete mobile workbench with no crop, blank state, placeholder, overlapping chrome, or vague entry point.

### 60-second daily job

Ask: who is this for, what requires attention now, why does it matter, and what can they do next? The answer must be visible without opening a brand story or reading hidden notes.

### 3-minute connected journey

Perform one main job and two supporting jobs. Each must lead to a new route, a changed state, a native handoff with a return state, or a visible recorded result. Customer/task/appointment identity must remain coherent across pages.

## What to capture

Capture home, C360, and task-detail views plus a screenshot before and after each of three state-changing interactions. Write observations of what was actually visible, not claims about the intended implementation.

## Score dimensions

- **Operating clarity:** daily work and next action are understandable.
- **Interaction reliability:** primary controls actually work and give a meaningful result.
- **Brand expression:** brand direction improves hierarchy without inventing the workflow.
- **Presentation readiness:** the prototype can carry a short review without visible defects.

A score below 4 is a delivery blocker. Screenshot evidence and browser observation outweigh a self-authored token or review note.
